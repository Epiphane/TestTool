2.3.4 Searching For Questions

If the raw list of questions is too overwhelming, the instructor can click the "Question Search" button in the lower left of the question table to open the search screen.

Figure 2: Question Bank Search Screen.

TestTool displays the search screen with the "Subjects" tabs activated. This view is organized into three tabs of increasing specificity, which are "Subjects", "Tests", and "Questions".

Once in the search screen, the instructor can return to the table of questions by clicking the "Question Table" button in the lower left. This screen also supports sorting the listed items by either item name, item creation date, or item modification date. The instructor clicks "Name" under "Sort by:".

Figure 3: Question Bank - Sorted by Name.

TestTool reorders the subjects in alphabetical order.

The instructor clicks Once the instructor clicks a subject, the test tab will be focused and tests belonging to the selected subject will be shown, and the subject name will be populated in the search bar. The instructor can also select the test tab manually, or clear out the search bar and click search to view all tests, as shown in Figure 4.

Figure 4: Question Bank - Tests.

Once again, the instructor can search or scroll for a test, and selecting a test will open the questions tab with the selected test name prefilled in the search bar, shown in Figure 5. Clearing the search bar and clicking search will remove the subject/test filter for the questions and will list all questions. The instructor once again can manually select the question tab to view all questions unfiltered.
